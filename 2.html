Comprehensive Report

Executive Summary

This report analyzes a dataset of 1,470 employees to identify attrition drivers.

The data shows that attrition is primarily driven by overtime, with the highest risk concentrated in Sales employees (38%).

Income disparity acts as an amplifier, increasing the likelihood of resignation when combined with workload pressure.

The recommended action is to restructure sales workload and conduct targeted salary adjustments.
Data & Methodology

Dataset: IBM HR (1,470 rows, 35 columns)
Tools: Power BI, DAX
Approach: Rates and averages instead of raw counts, with cross-analysis (Department + Overtime)
  • Overtime employees show 31% attrition
  • Non-overtime only 10%
  • Workload is the strongest observable factor
  • Leavers earn $4,899 on average
  • Stayers earn $6,669
  • 26.5% income gap increases risk
  • HR attrition: 15%
  • Sales: 14%
  • R&D lowest at 9%

High Risk Segment

Sales employees under overtime reach 38% attrition (128 out of 446)

Recommendations
1. Sales Quota Review
Investigate workload pressure causing excessive overtime.
2. Overtime Restructuring
Introduce incentive-based or optional overtime.
3. Salary Adjustment
Target employees below $5,000 exposed to overtime.
